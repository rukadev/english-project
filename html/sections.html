Reflection

In the "Heritage" level, the character follows a path of crystals, which represents his journey of following this new path that he has chosen for himself. Originally leading a comfortable life working in a library in Buenos Aires, this level is set in a relaxing town to represent this lifestyle. Despite this though, Juan embarks on a new path within his life, which, based on the quote, he chooses due to his romanticization with southern life through admiration for his grandfather. This level marks the beginning of Juan's journey.

In the "Arrival" level, the character builds a bridge to get from one place to another. When on the train going to the south, Juan takes note of the change of scenery during his ride. The signifiance of the bridge marks the transition from life in Bueons Aires, to the unfamiliar landscape of the southern prarie. The bridge acts to reinforce this change, representing the arrival from one environment to the next. Juan's captivation with this scenery through descriptive detail in the quote demonstrates the hold through which fantasy has on his perception of reality.
